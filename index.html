METABOLIC TOKYO

VR Exhibition
(under construction)

Metabolic Tokyo

×

Metabolism (新陳代謝) was a Japanese architectural movement that called for radical design approaches to support organic urban changes witnessed by Tokyo's postwar reconstruction from ashes. After decades of shifting urban tensions and conflicts, the avant-garde plans of Metabolic Tokyo are left to an illusory future of the absolute past that only appeals to architectural historians and a few tourists at the to-be-demolished Nagakin Capsule Tower designed by the Metabolist Kisho Kurokawa. What if, in an alternate chronotype without the power shifts of the 1973 Oil Crisis, Metabolists implemented their Tokyo plans at full scales with the political supports they fought for? Are we going to arrive at the same positions in the pandemic, climate changes, elderly society, and urban surveillance by living in Kurokawa's agricultural grids, commuting on Kenzo Tange's Tokyo Bay Spine, and working between Araka Isozaki's clustered cubes?

This project aims to communicate the historical thoughts of Metabolism to non-architectural audiences by reassembling Metabolist projects into first-person VR experiences of Tokyo urban routines in an alternate present developed from Metabolism. The digital exhibition will allow audiences to inhabit the Metabolist urban space, navigate their day through interaction with people and artifacts, and draw alternative perspectives on cities and everyday life in the face of contemporary urban challenges.
