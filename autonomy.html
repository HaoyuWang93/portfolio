AUTONOMY ON THE EDGE

The 20th-Century image of California dream often associates with a widely criticized built form of vehicle-oriented transportation and monotonous residential neighborhoods sprawling toward an infinite suburbia. Nowadays, emerging technologies of renewable energy and artificial intelligence is framing the concept of suburbia for future live and work. By the 2050s when ‘level five’ autonomous mobility systems and unmanned production lines proliferate, we might reconsider the idea of California dream in a provocative manner.

On the north of Palm Springs, the vast desert landscape is being exploited for wind harvesting and sporadic housing developments. Instead of looking forward to another residential colony of adjacent cities, we would like this region to be the site of an autonomous future which is self-reliant, well integrated with new opportunities for economic and social wellbeing. Autonomy on the Edge re-invents suburbia with strong internal activities and diverse programs achieved by the integration of work and live.
[Project of MIT Urban Design Studio, Fall 2018, Teammate: Piyush Verma, Instructors: Alan Berger, Rafi Segal, Jonah Susskind]
